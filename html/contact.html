FAQs

Q1)How is TEDx different from a TED conference?

TED conferences are organized and run directly by TED. TEDx events are fully planned and coordinated independently. The goal of TEDX is to inspire a passion for spreading ideas from within local communities.

Q2)How much do we need to pay for the ticket?

150/- per person. Early bird passes available for 100/-

Q3)Can we return the tickets?

No, you can’t

Q4)When and where will the TEDxDSEC 1.0 event take place?

It is planned to take place in the month of november on a virtual platform ie., zoom.

Q5)Are kids allowed? Or is there any age restriction?

No, there’s no age restriction. Anyone who wishes to attend can buy the ticket.

Q6)Are your speakers also curated?

Yes, our speakers are also curated. What you see on the day of the event are carefully curated and highly rehearsed talks. We have an internal curation team that works with the speaker to create the draft and then final cut of their talk.

Q7)Where can I contact for more queries?

You can DM us on instagram or put your query in the form below. The team will surely look into the problem.

Q8)Can I organize a TEDx event in my university or town?

Yes. You need to know the TEDx rules and fill in the form for getting a license from the central TED office. The event can be held within one street, region, school, university or town. The standard license allows to hold an event for up to 100 guests.
